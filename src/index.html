Rear Admiral Grace Murray Hopper

A programming pioneer known as "Grandma COBOL".

Hopper pictured alongside colleagues at the UNIVAC I console, c. 1960.

A timeline of Grace Hopper's Life

  • December 9, 1906 - born in New York ity, New York
  • 1928 - Graduated from Vassar College where she studied both math & physics
  • 1930 - Graduated from Yale University with Masters Degree in mathematics
  • 1931 - began teaching at Vassar while also continuing to study at Yale
  • 1934 - became the first woman to earn a Ph.D. in mathematics from Yale University
  • 1943 - joined the US Naval Reserve and served during WWII, and was assigned Bureau of Ordnance Computation Project at Harvard University, where she learned to program a Mark I computer. SHe later worked on the MArk II and aMArk III computers as well.
  • 1949 - moved into the private industry working at Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation, then with Remington Rand, where she oversaw programming for the UNIVAC computer.
  • 1952 - Grace and her team created the first compiler for computer languages. This compiler was a precursor for the Common Business Oriented Language, or COBOL, a widely adapted language that would be used around the world. Though she did not invent COBOL, Hopper encouraged its adaptation.
  • 1966 - Grace retired from the Naval Reserve
  • 1991 - became the first female individual recipient of the National Medal of Technology
  • 1986 - When she retired from the U.S. Navy, at age 79, Grace was the oldest serving officer in the service
  • 1986 - Grace appeared on television with David Letterman, during which she explained nanoseconds and picoseconds to Letterman
  • January 1, 1992 - At the age of 85, she died in Arlington, Virginia. She was laid to rest in the Arlington National Cemetery.
  • 1997 - The USS Hopper, a guided missile destroyer, was commissioned in Grace Hopper's honor
  • 2004 - the University of Missouri honored Hopper with a computer museum on its campus, dubbed “Grace’s Place.”
  • 2013 - Hopper was remembered with a "Google Doodle" on her birthday
  • 2016 - Hopper posthumously honored with the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Barack Obama
